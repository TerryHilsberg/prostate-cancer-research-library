# Prostate Cancer Research Library

This is a curated, patient-perspective research interface for **prostate cancer diagnosis and treatment decisions**.

!!! warning "Not medical advice"
    This repository summarizes research for education and clinician discussion. It does not diagnose, prescribe, recommend a treatment, or replace medical care.

## Purpose

The repository is designed to help a potential patient understand:

- What tests and diagnostic steps usually mean
- How clinicians classify risk and stage disease
- Treatment options and trade-offs
- Side effects and quality-of-life outcomes
- When active surveillance may be considered
- How new evidence may change questions to ask clinicians

## Default evidence hierarchy

1. Current clinical guidelines and consensus statements from reputable oncology/urology bodies
2. Randomized controlled trials and high-quality meta-analyses
3. Large prospective cohorts and registry studies
4. Diagnostic accuracy studies with clinically relevant outcomes
5. Patient-reported outcome studies
6. Mechanistic, preclinical, or hypothesis-generating studies, clearly labeled

## Core patient questions

- Is cancer present, and how certain is that conclusion?
- If present, how aggressive does it appear to be?
- What are the reasonable management options for this risk group?
- What are the likely benefits, harms, uncertainties, and quality-of-life effects?
- What would change the decision later?
