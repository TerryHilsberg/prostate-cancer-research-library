# Shared Decision-Making

## Scope

Research notes and filed articles for **shared decision-making**.

## Key patient questions

- What does this evidence help a patient understand?
- Which risk group or clinical situation does it apply to?
- What are the benefits, harms, and uncertainties?
- What should be discussed with a qualified clinician?
- Would a risk calculator, biomarker, genomic classifier, AI model, or imaging-fusion result actually change management?

## Filed items

- [AI and Integrated Test Decision Support in Prostate Cancer: Evidence Map](../papers/ai-integrated-prostate-cancer-decision-support-evidence/index.md) — patient-facing overview of how PSA, mpMRI, biopsy/pathology, PSMA PET/CT, biomarkers, risk calculators, radiomics, and AI can support shared decisions about biopsy, staging, local treatment, focal targeting, radiotherapy boost, ADT use/duration, and salvage therapy.

## Shared-decision cautions

- A decision-support tool is useful only if it applies to the patient's risk group and would change a real decision.
- Prognostic tools estimate risk; predictive tools estimate treatment benefit. They should not be confused.
- AI outputs need external validation, clinician explanation, and integration with patient priorities such as continence, erectile function, bowel effects, anxiety, treatment burden, and tolerance for uncertainty.
