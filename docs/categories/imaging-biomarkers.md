# Imaging & Biomarkers

## Scope

Research notes and filed articles for **imaging & biomarkers**.

## Key patient questions

- What does this evidence help a patient understand?
- Which risk group or clinical situation does it apply to?
- What are the benefits, harms, and uncertainties?
- What should be discussed with a qualified clinician?

## Filed items

- [AI and Integrated Test Decision Support in Prostate Cancer: Evidence Map](../papers/ai-integrated-prostate-cancer-decision-support-evidence/index.md) — overview of how PSA, mpMRI, biopsy/pathology, PSMA PET/CT, blood/genomic biomarkers, risk calculators, radiomics, and digital pathology AI can be combined for biopsy decisions, lesion targeting, staging, treatment intensity, and local-treatment planning.
- [2026-07-13 weekly scan](../updates/2026-07-13.md) — Imaging/biomarker watch: real-world MRI-before-biopsy evidence supports MRI as a strong risk-stratification tool but not a rule-out test in all contexts; PSMA PET and MRI-based AI models for recurrence prediction remain promising but should be treated as validation-dependent decision supports rather than stand-alone treatment selectors.
- [2026-07-06 weekly scan](../updates/2026-07-06.md) — Imaging/biomarker watch: EAU 2026 notes updated imaging recommendations for biopsy indication, staging, biochemical recurrence, and risk calculators; the FUPERMAN prospective study raises PSMA PET/CT-targeted biopsy as a possible future pathway after PI-RADS 2–3 MRI, with current limitations from small single-center evidence.
- [PSMA PET/CT in Prostate Cancer: Evidence Map](../papers/psma-pet-ct-prostate-cancer-evidence/index.md) — evidence summary on PSMA PET/CT as a decision-changing imaging tool across initial diagnosis-adjacent evaluation, high-risk staging, biochemical recurrence, treatment selection, and selected monitoring questions.
- [Hormone Levels and Prostate Cancer Incidence: Evidence Map](../papers/hormone-levels-prostate-cancer-incidence-evidence/index.md) — biomarker/risk-context evidence on testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, LH, IGF-I, and prostate cancer incidence/aggressive-disease risk.
- [Genetic and Epigenetic Background to Prostate Cancer Risk: Evidence Map](../papers/genetic-epigenetic-risk-prostate-cancer-evidence/index.md) — inherited genes, polygenic scores, tumour sequencing, and epigenetic biomarkers as risk and precision-medicine tools.
