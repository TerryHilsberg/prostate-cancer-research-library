# Nutrition

## Scope

Research notes and filed articles about **nutrition, diet quality, body composition, metabolic health, and prostate cancer diagnosis/treatment outcomes**.

This category is for patient-facing evidence on nutrition as a supportive health factor, not as a substitute for clinically indicated cancer diagnosis or treatment.

## Key patient questions

- What dietary patterns are associated with prostate cancer risk, progression, recurrence, or treatment tolerance?
- What is known about body weight, body composition, insulin resistance, cardiometabolic health, and outcomes?
- Are there nutrition considerations during active surveillance, surgery, radiation, ADT, chemotherapy, or survivorship?
- Which claims are supported by human clinical evidence, and which remain speculative?
- What should be discussed with an oncologist, urologist, dietitian, or GP?

## Evidence to prioritize

- Human randomized trials and high-quality meta-analyses
- Large prospective cohort studies with clinically meaningful endpoints
- Studies of diet quality, Mediterranean-style patterns, protein adequacy, weight change, metabolic syndrome, diabetes, and cardiovascular risk in prostate cancer populations
- Patient-reported outcomes and treatment-tolerance studies
- Nutrition guidance from reputable cancer organizations

## Cautions

!!! warning "Not medical advice"
    Nutrition notes are educational and should not be used to delay diagnosis, avoid treatment, or replace advice from a qualified clinician.

Be especially cautious with:

- High-dose supplements or concentrated extracts
- Claims that a diet can cure cancer
- Major weight-loss or fasting programs during active treatment
- Interactions between supplements and surgery, radiation, ADT, chemotherapy, anticoagulants, or other medications
