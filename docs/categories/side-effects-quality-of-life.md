## Filed items

- [2026-07-13 weekly scan](../updates/2026-07-13.md) — Supportive-care watch: a service evaluation found that comprehensive geriatric assessment for older men starting ADT was feasible and identified many ADT-related and non-ADT interventions; this supports asking about bone health, falls/frailty, cognition, cardiovascular/metabolic risk, medications, nutrition, exercise, and social support before or soon after ADT.
- [2026-07-06 weekly scan](../updates/2026-07-06.md) — Side-effect/safety watch: a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized trials found increased hypokalemia risk with androgen receptor axis-targeted therapy, especially abiraterone-containing regimens; patients should discuss potassium, blood pressure, edema/fluid retention, liver-test, steroid, cardiac, kidney, and interacting-medication monitoring with clinicians.
- [Blood Pressure, Hypertension, and Prostate Cancer: Evidence Map](../papers/blood-pressure-prostate-cancer-evidence/index.md) — review of blood pressure/hypertension in prostate cancer, including incidence/fatal risk signals, uncertain recurrence evidence, cardiovascular survivorship, and treatment-related hypertension during ADT and androgen-receptor pathway therapy.
- [Immune Modulation and Anti-inflammatory Agents in Prostate Cancer: Evidence Map](../papers/immune-modulation-anti-inflammatory-prostate-cancer-evidence/index.md) — safety-oriented discussion of immune-related adverse events, aspirin/NSAID bleeding and kidney/cardiovascular risks, celecoxib risks, corticosteroid effects, vitamin D correction, and supplement/LDN interaction questions.

## Patient-reported outcomes resources

### PCOR-ANZ Patient Experiences with Prostate Cancer — Summary Report 2024

- **Source:** Prostate Cancer Outcomes Registry – Australia and New Zealand (PCOR-ANZ)
- **Document:** [Patient Experiences with Prostate Cancer: PCOR-ANZ Summary Report 2024](https://prostatecancerregistry.org/pcor_cms/media/reports/MCS-4813%20PCOR-ANZ%20Annual%20Report%20Summary_DIGI_FA2.pdf)
- **Evidence type:** Registry-based patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) summary.
- **Population:** Men diagnosed with prostate cancer between 2020–2022 and participating in PCOR-ANZ; the registry includes Australia and New Zealand data.
- **Why it belongs here:** This report is directly about side effects and lived experience 12 months after treatment or observation, focusing on urinary function, bowel function, sexual wellbeing, and lack of energy.

**Patient-facing summary:** PCOR-ANZ collects clinical information plus direct patient-reported outcomes. The 2024 summary is useful when comparing likely quality-of-life effects of active surveillance, surgery, radiation therapy, radiation plus ADT, and ADT with/without chemotherapy across prostate cancer risk groups.

**Selected 12-month patient-reported outcome examples from the report:**

| Clinical situation | Urinary / bowel outcomes | Sexual wellbeing / energy outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| Low-risk disease after active surveillance | 3% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 7% reported more than one urinary leak/day | 23% reported sexual function as a problem; 10% reported lack of energy as a problem |
| Low-risk disease after surgery | 27% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 17% reported more than one urinary leak/day | 47% reported sexual function as a problem; 33% could have a fair–very good erection |
| Intermediate-risk disease after surgery | 29% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 19% reported more than one urinary leak/day | 46% reported sexual function as a problem; 25% could have a fair–very good erection |
| Intermediate-risk disease after radiation plus ADT | 6% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 9% reported more than one urinary leak/day; 8% reported bowel habits as a problem | 40% reported sexual function as a problem; 25% reported lack of energy as a problem |
| High-risk disease after radiation plus ADT | 11% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 12% reported more than one urinary leak/day; 10% reported bowel habits as a problem | 38% reported sexual function as a problem; 34% reported lack of energy as a problem |
| Advanced disease after ADT with/without chemotherapy | 10% needed more than one urinary pad/day; 11% reported more than one urinary leak/day | 34% reported sexual function as a problem; 36% reported lack of energy as a problem |

**Questions for clinician:**

- For my risk group and treatment options, what urinary, bowel, sexual, and energy outcomes are most likely at 12 months?
- How do these registry outcomes compare with outcomes at this hospital or with this clinician?
- What can be done before and after treatment to reduce urinary incontinence, bowel symptoms, erectile dysfunction, and fatigue?
- Would pelvic-floor physiotherapy, sexual rehabilitation, exercise physiology, psychological support, or specialist nursing support be appropriate?

!!! note "Interpretation caveat"
    Registry summaries are very useful for real-world expectations, but they do not replace individual risk estimates. Outcomes depend on cancer risk group, age, baseline urinary/sexual/bowel function, comorbidities, treatment details, clinician/team experience, and personal priorities.
