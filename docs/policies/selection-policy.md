# Selection Policy

## Include by default

- New or updated guidelines from NCCN, EAU, AUA, ASCO, NICE, USPSTF, Cancer Australia, or major cancer centers
- Randomized trials, meta-analyses, and large prospective studies relevant to prostate cancer diagnosis, treatment, surveillance, or outcomes
- Diagnostic accuracy studies with clear clinical endpoints
- Patient-reported outcome studies for urinary, sexual, bowel, fatigue, mental health, or survivorship outcomes
- Safety updates that may affect clinical conversations

## Exclude or down-rank

- Preclinical-only studies unless clearly practice-relevant
- Small retrospective single-center studies without a patient-facing implication
- Press releases without accessible study details
- Opinion pieces not tied to evidence or guideline changes
- Commercial claims without independent validation

## Scoring rubric

Score 0–10. Include items scoring **>= 7.5** or items with lower score only if they are important safety/guideline watchlist items.

| Dimension | Points |
|---|---:|
| Evidence quality | 0–3 |
| Patient decision relevance | 0–2 |
| Recency/novelty | 0–1 |
| Applicability to real clinical pathways | 0–2 |
| Clarity of risks/limitations | 0–1 |
| Source credibility | 0–1 |

## Required note format

- Citation/title/authors/date/source URL
- Evidence type and population
- Category/categories
- Summary
- Main finding
- Patient relevance
- Limitations
- Safety/clinical cautions
- Whether it changes a category page or decision map
