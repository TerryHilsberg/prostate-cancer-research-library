# Glossary

- **PSA**: Prostate-specific antigen, a blood marker that can rise for cancer and non-cancer reasons.
- **PSA density**: PSA divided by prostate volume; can help interpret PSA in context.
- **mpMRI**: Multiparametric MRI, used to identify suspicious prostate lesions.
- **PI-RADS**: MRI scoring system for likelihood of clinically significant cancer.
- **Biopsy**: Tissue sampling to diagnose cancer and determine grade.
- **Gleason score / Grade Group**: Pathology grading of cancer aggressiveness.
- **Active surveillance**: Structured monitoring for lower-risk cancer, with treatment if risk changes.
- **Radical prostatectomy**: Surgical removal of the prostate.
- **Radiation therapy**: Treatment using targeted radiation, including external beam or brachytherapy.
- **ADT**: Androgen deprivation therapy, systemic treatment that lowers androgen signaling.
- **PSMA PET**: Imaging test that can detect prostate cancer spread or recurrence in selected contexts.
- **mCRPC**: Metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer, meaning metastatic disease that is progressing despite castrate-level androgen suppression.
- **MSI-H/dMMR**: Microsatellite instability-high or mismatch-repair deficient tumor biology; uncommon in prostate cancer but can make immune checkpoint therapy more relevant.
- **Sipuleucel-T**: An autologous cellular immunotherapy with randomized survival evidence in selected asymptomatic or minimally symptomatic mCRPC.
- **Immune checkpoint inhibitor**: A drug such as pembrolizumab, nivolumab, atezolizumab, or ipilimumab that alters immune braking signals; benefits in prostate cancer are usually biomarker-selected or investigational.
