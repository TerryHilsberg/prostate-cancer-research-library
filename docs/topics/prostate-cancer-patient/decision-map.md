# Decision Map

## A. Before diagnosis

- What prompted testing?
- What is the PSA trend, PSA density, and age-adjusted context?
- Is MRI appropriate before biopsy?
- What are the false-positive, false-negative, and overdiagnosis issues?

## B. If biopsy is being considered

- Transperineal vs transrectal approach
- MRI-targeted plus systematic biopsy vs systematic alone
- Infection, bleeding, urinary retention, and pain risks
- Whether antibiotics or anesthesia/sedation are relevant

## C. If cancer is found

- Grade Group/Gleason score
- Number and percentage of positive cores
- PSA, PSA density, MRI lesion size, clinical stage
- Whether genomic classifier or PSMA PET is relevant

## D. Management options

- Active surveillance
- Radical prostatectomy
- External beam radiation therapy
- Brachytherapy
- Focal therapy/investigational approaches
- ADT or systemic therapy for higher-risk/advanced disease

## E. Outcomes to track

- Cancer control and metastasis-free survival
- Need for later treatment
- Urinary incontinence/irritative symptoms
- Sexual function
- Bowel symptoms
- Fatigue, mood, and quality of life
